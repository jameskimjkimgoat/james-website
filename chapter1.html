What if there were novels with protagonists who live every moment of their lives like skydivers who momentarily engage in high-risk leisure activities? Well, two of the most influential writers of this topic are Jerzy Kozinski and J.D. Salinger. Kozinski's Being There and Salinger's Catcher in the Rye both explore individuals who live life like a reckless game, and go wherever their heart takes them. While Being There focuses on the internal turmoil that happens to the protagonist as he figures out the world with the brain age of a child, Catcher in the Rye focuses both on the physical and internal journey of Holden Caulfield as he thugs out the whole situation after getting expelled.
